A bit of history

Sewing is the craft fastening or attaching objects using stitches made with a needle and thread. Sewing is one of the oldest of the textile arts, arising in the Paleolithic era. For thousands of years sewing was done by hand. The invention if the sewing machine in the 19th century and the rise of computerization in the 20th century led to mass production and export of sewn objects.

The Industrial Revolution shifted the production of textiles from the household to the mills. By the early 1840's, sewing machines began to appear. Sewing underwnt further developments during the 20th century. As sewing machines began more affordable to the working class, demand for sewing patterns grew.

Important dates

  • 4000 BC: weaving of clothes.
  • 1790: first sewing machine patent by Thomas Saint.
  • 1850: Isaac Singer developed the first sewing machines that could operate quickly and accurately and surpass the productivity of a seamstress or tailor sewing by hand.

The impact of fashion on the environment

As the years go by and social media is more and more part of our lifes, we become accustomed to buying things that never last for very long. Clothes don't miss out! We see them on someone we follow, we are bombarded by advertisement that tell us that we shouldn't be content with what we already have so we end up buying and buying to fill in an empty space and we never wear them because they didn' make us happier. What people don't realise is that the fashion industry is the second largest polluter in the world, just after the oil industry.

How?

  • Water pollution.
  • Water consumption.
  • Microfibers in our oceans.
  • Wastes accumulation.
  • Chemicals Addiction.
  • Greenhouse gases emissions.
  • Soils degradation and desertification.
  • Rainforest destruction.

What can we do about it?

  • We need to buy clothes made in countries with stricter environmental regulations for factories such as the EU
  • We need to choose organic fibers and natural fibers that do not require chemicals to be produced.
  • We need to choose fibers with low water consumption such as linen, recycled fibers.
  • We need to mend our old clothes instead of buying new ones.
  • We need to make our own clothes with natural fibers to increase the chances of wearing what we make instead of throwing what we buy.
